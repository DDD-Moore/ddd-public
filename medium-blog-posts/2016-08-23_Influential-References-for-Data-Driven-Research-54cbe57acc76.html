Influential References for Data-Driven Research

By Carly Strasser

Back in 2014, the Olympic torch was in Sochi instead of Rio, everything was awesome, and the Data-Driven Discovery Initiative at the Moore Foundation was just getting warmed up. We ran an investigator competition to select 14 Data-Driven Discovery Investigators to receive $1.5M each over five years to fund their research. We ended up with a fantastic group that continues to wow me with their achievements, but this post isn’t about them — it’s about what influences researchers like them.

The Foundation had about ~1100 researchers apply for the investigator awards, and all of them provided a list of up to five “influential works” as part of their application. This provided quite the treasure trove of data on what data-driven researchers think are the most important works for data science. Moore Fellow Mark Stalzer and DDD Program Director Chris Mentzel recognized the potential importance of this information, and recently published a paper reviewing the works identified by the applicants.

Stalzer and Mentzel found that out of the nearly 5000 references, there were 53 works that were cited at least six times. Their Table 1 lists the 22 works cited 10 or more times, and could be used as a reading list for an intro data science course. It’s an great mix of works describing tools (BLAST, PageRank), statistical methods (Random Forests, Bootstrap methods), and fundamental ways of thinking (pattern recognition, fourth paradigm, probabilistic reasoning). I’ve got big plans to work my way through the list of 22 by the end of 2017 — wish me luck!

The paper describes how Stalzer and Mentzel clustered the significant works, and provides some insight into broader landscape of the clusters as they relate to data-driven research. It’s an interesting read for anyone thinking about a career in data science, or for those that want to beef up their skills for data-driven research. You can read the full article at SpringerPlus:

Stalzer and Mentzel, 2016. “A preliminary review of influential works in data-driven discovery”. SpringerPlus 5:1266. DOI: 10.1186/s40064–016–2888–8

If you want the tl;dr version, I’ve collected a list of the 71 most cited references from the data set here: http://www.citeulike.org/user/strasser/tag/influentialpapers.
