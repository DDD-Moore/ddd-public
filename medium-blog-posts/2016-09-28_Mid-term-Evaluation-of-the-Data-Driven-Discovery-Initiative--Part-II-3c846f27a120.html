Mid-term Evaluation of the Data-Driven Discovery Initiative: Part II

by Chris Mentzel

A couple of weeks ago, I posted about the origins of the Data-Driven Discovery Initiative, and outlined what we are funding and why. In this post, I dive into the structure of the initiative, our anticipated gains from a mid-term evaluation that’s just beginning, and how that information will feed into a new strategic plan for 2018 and beyond.

What is DDD trying to accomplish?

The goal of the Data-Driven Discovery (DDD) Initiative is to catalyze shifts towards new norms in academia, where researchers are rewarded for their work in data-driven science, and where new tools and practices for handling large and complex data are widely used. We think these shifts will enable scientists to leverage modern-scale scientific data to drive new types of discoveries.

To achieve this goal, DDD has three integrated strategies:

Institutions Strategy — Demonstrate innovative organizational structures and activities within academic institutions (“Data Science Environments”) which foster data-driven scientists and practices.
People Strategy — Reinforce data scientists’ value in academia by providing resources to investigators for their labs to practice their innovative, data-driven research.
Practices Strategy — Drive the creation and dissemination of readily usable and innovative tools, knowledge, and techniques for engaging in data-driven research.

Research Questions for the Evaluation

The Moore Foundation’s Measurement, Evaluation, and Learning team has hired Abt Associates to conduct the independent, external evaluation of the DDD Initiative. The external evaluators will study the initiative as a whole, as well as each strategic area, with the following research questions in mind:

  • Thus far, what results has the initiative had, both intended and unintended?
  • What has the interplay been among the three strategies, and have they been effective?
  • What progress or growth in the data-driven science landscape has DDD’s work most likely contributed to?

Ultimately, the report from the external evaluators will be published as a public document, and the DDD team will post a response to that report after our Board has made a decision as to what might come next.

How will the report be used?

The DDD team will combine the evaluation report with our own understanding of the state of the field to synthesize a new strategic plan (see Part I for how we do this). We will also request input from our Science Advisory Board, the academic data science community, and industry data science organizations that intersect with our research community. In other words, like our first plan, any new strategic plan will continue to take advantage of the wisdom of the community.

The new strategic plan will then be presented to our Board of Trustees who will then determine what’s next for DDD. Under the current time frame this pivotal board meeting will happen in November 2017.

DDD started from an identified need in the community to support the people and practice of data-driven science. The intent of this mid-term evaluation is to help refresh our understanding of the current needs and identify ways to continue advancing data-driven research in the natural sciences.
